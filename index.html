Are movie critics actually as critical of movies as they say?
Are audiences hard to please or are critics just too easily pleased?

For this bar chart I have split every movie in a year into one of 2 catagories,
movies where the critic score is higher than the audience score, and movies where
the audience score is greater than the critic score. After this, I counted the amount of
movies in each of those catagories that won an oscar and plotted that. From this we see that
movies that have higher critic ratings are far more likely to win an oscar than movies that
have higher audience ratings.

I found the difference in the bar graph really intersting. So to investigate I made this lollipop graph. In the graph I have
plotted the number of movies where the critic score is higher than the audience score, and the number of movies where
the audience score is greater than the critic score. We can see that critic ratings for movies is far often higher than the audience
rating for movies.
